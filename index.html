Research

My research focuses on advancing 3D imaging through next-generation sensor technologies and machine learning. Currently, I am developing methods to improve time-of-flight 3D imaging using single-photon avalanche diode (SPAD) cameras, aiming to make high-resolution depth sensing more accurate and efficient. In my undergrad, I developed supervised transformer vision models for the reconstruction of sinograms.

Hardware-Aware Coding Design for Compressive Single-Photon 3D Cameras
David Parra, Advisors: Andreas Velten, Mohit Gupta
ICCP, 2025 (under submission)
Paper / Supplement

We propose optimized coding and illumination strategies that jointly consider hardware constraints and photon statistics to improve depth reconstruction quality in compressive single-photon imaging.

Fast Flash LiDAR Using Single-Photon Gated Cameras
David Parra, Advisors: Andreas Velten, Mohit Gupta
In preparation
Manuscript

We explore photon-efficient gating and computational methods for real-time flash LiDAR imaging, enabling high-resolution 3D reconstructions under challenging illumination and dynamic scenes.
